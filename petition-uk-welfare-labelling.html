Mandate Method-of-Production Welfare Labelling on UK Meat, Dairy and Eggs

UK egg packaging already carries a one-to-four method-of-production code at point of sale: caged, barn, free-range, or organic. Meat and dairy do not. Consumers who want to avoid the worst confinement systems cannot tell from a label whether they are buying from a cage, crate, or higher-welfare system — even though the UK slaughters roughly 1.1 billion animals a year, the overwhelming majority in intensive systems.

What the data shows: UK slaughter statistics are published monthly by DEFRA via the cattle, sheep, and pig slaughter dataset and poultry statistics dataset. Together they document ~1 billion broiler chickens, tens of millions of hens, ~10 million pigs, and ~15 million sheep killed for food in the UK each year.
Where the labelling gap is: The only mandatory welfare labelling in the UK today is the egg marketing regulations retained from EU Regulation 589/2008. Calls to extend method-of-production labelling to pig, poultry and dairy products have been raised by the cross-party APPG on Animal Welfare and supported by organisations including Compassion in World Farming and the RSPCA as part of their ongoing honest-labelling advocacy.
What to ask DEFRA for:
  1. Extend method-of-production labelling beyond eggs to pork, chicken, dairy and composite products, using the same one-to-four scheme already proven at retail.
  2. Require a legible label on every pack at point of sale — no hiding behind brand assurance schemes or per-retailer codes.
  3. Publish a timeline with consultation, legislation and enforcement dates, so the reform doesn't stall in perpetual consideration.
