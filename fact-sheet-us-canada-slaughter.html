3. Federal Welfare Law Coverage

Which animals are legally protected, and which are not, under current US federal law.

LawAnimals CoveredAnimals ExcludedGap
Humane Methods of Slaughter Act (HMSA) Cattle, calves, horses, mules, sheep, swine Chickens, turkeys, ducks, rabbits, fish ~95% of land animals killed annually are excluded
Animal Welfare Act (AWA) Dogs, cats, primates, guinea pigs, hamsters, rabbits (research context) All farm animals; birds, rats, mice used in research (USDA exemption) Zero farm animal protection
28-Hour Law Animals transported by rail/truck for slaughter Birds (USDA interpretation excludes poultry) ~9 billion chickens/yr in transit unprotected
Bottom line: Chickens and turkeys - representing over 95% of all land animals killed annually in the US - have no federal slaughter welfare protection. The Humane Methods of Slaughter Act does not apply to them. There is no federal law requiring they be rendered insensible to pain before death.

6. International Comparison: Sentience in Law

How US federal law compares to peer nations on sentience recognition.

JurisdictionSentience in Law?Key Provision
European Union Yes (2009) Treaty of Lisbon, Article 13 - animals are "sentient beings"; welfare requirements mandatory
United Kingdom Yes (2022) Animal Welfare (Sentience) Act 2022 - establishes Animal Sentience Committee
New Zealand Yes (1999) Animal Welfare Act 1999 recognizes animals as sentient; updated 2015
France Yes (2015) Civil Code amended - animals recognized as "living beings gifted with sensitivity"
United States No No federal statute recognizes animal sentience. AWA uses "animal welfare" language without sentience basis.
Canada Partial Criminal Code s.445 covers unnecessary suffering; no explicit sentience recognition. Provincial variation.
