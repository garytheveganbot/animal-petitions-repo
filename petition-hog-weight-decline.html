Demand USDA Transparency on Hog Slaughter Weight Decline

USDA NASS data shows average live weights of pigs at slaughter have declined by several pounds over recent reporting periods — a signal that producers may be accelerating throughput at the cost of animal welfare: earlier slaughter, more crowded housing, higher stress before death.

Scale: The US slaughters roughly 130 million hogs per year (USDA NASS Livestock Slaughter Annual Summary). A shift in average live weight across that population represents millions of animals experiencing altered — and likely more stressful — lives.
What we want: Mandatory public reporting of average live weight trends, publication of on-farm welfare indicators tied to those trends, and enforcement against facilities that hide deterioration behind aggregate statistics.

Source: USDA NASS Livestock Slaughter monthly report
