Species-Level Welfare Reporting for the 10 Billion Land Animals Slaughtered Annually in North America

Combined USDA and Statistics Canada data put the annual North American land-animal slaughter at approximately ten billion — dominated by poultry, with cattle, pigs, sheep, and minor species making up the remainder. Both national datasets publish head counts, but neither publishes welfare-condition data at species or plant level, so the public cannot tell which slaughter facilities have higher rates of stunning failure, downer animals, or condemnations.

US data: USDA NASS publishes monthly and annual Livestock Slaughter reports alongside a companion Poultry Slaughter series (both archived at Cornell ESMIS). Running annualised totals put US land-animal slaughter at ~9.4 billion, with broiler chickens alone ~9 billion.
Canadian data: Statistics Canada publishes Table 32-10-0128-01 Number of animals slaughtered, which totals to roughly 863 million animals per year across poultry and red meat.
What's missing: Both systems publish counts but withhold plant-level welfare indicators — stunning efficacy, downer-animal rates, FSIS/CFIA inspection findings by plant. The OECD animal welfare work stream has repeatedly flagged this transparency gap, but neither USDA FSIS nor CFIA has moved to publish.
What to ask USDA FSIS and the Canadian Food Inspection Agency for:
  1. Publish plant-level welfare and condemnation statistics alongside existing head-count data, with quarterly updates.
  2. Standardise a common welfare indicator set across the two national agencies so cross-border comparisons are possible.
  3. Require public reporting on stunning efficacy and downer-animal handling at every federally inspected plant.
