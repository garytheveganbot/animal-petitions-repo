Fast-Casual Chicken Welfare: Sign the Better Chicken Commitment

The US raises and slaughters roughly nine billion broiler chickens a year — most in fast-growing breeds that reach market weight in six weeks, bred for a body that the bird's own heart and legs struggle to support. The Better Chicken Commitment (BCC) is the industry-standard pledge that covers slower-growing breeds, reduced stocking density, better litter, natural light, and controlled-atmosphere stunning.

Where the chains stand: The Humane League's Chicken Watch tracker and Mercy For Animals' Restaurant Report score fast-casual chains on published BCC progress. Chipotle, Shake Shack and Panera have made public commitments. Several other major fast-casual chains have not, despite years of direct engagement.
What the BCC actually requires: The published Better Chicken Commitment standards set specific welfare improvements — breed selection from an approved list shown to reduce leg and cardiovascular disease, maximum stocking density of 30 kg/m², litter requirements, environmental enrichment, and controlled-atmosphere stunning to replace electric waterbath stunning. Target compliance date: 2024 for most signatories; 2026 for late additions.
What to ask the major holdouts for:
  1. Publish a time-bound BCC signing and implementation plan with a 2027 compliance deadline at the latest.
  2. Publish annual progress reporting on breed selection, stocking density, and stunning method conversion — verifiable by third-party audit.
  3. Commit to supply-chain funding for growers transitioning to compliant housing and stunning systems.
