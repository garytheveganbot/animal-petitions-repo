Defend Michigan's Cage-Free Egg Law Against Federal Preemption

Michigan Public Act 132 of 2019 amended the Animal Industry Act to require that all shell eggs produced in Michigan and all eggs sold in Michigan come from cage-free housing systems, effective 1 January 2025. The law passed with bipartisan support. It sits in a line of state welfare laws including California's Proposition 12 and Massachusetts' Question 3, all of which the US Supreme Court upheld in National Pork Producers Council v. Ross (2023).

What's at stake: Approximately 300 million egg-laying hens are raised in the US each year; historically the majority in battery cages. State cage-free laws have driven the fastest industry shift toward cage-free housing seen to date — United Egg Producers data show cage-free flock share rising from 4% in 2010 to above 40% by 2024, largely driven by California/Michigan/Massachusetts-style statutes.
The threat: Federal legislative proposals in the vein of the EATS Act (Ending Agricultural Trade Suppression) would preempt these state laws — forcing Michigan back onto the federal baseline, which permits battery cages. See the Animal Legal Defense Fund's farmed-animals focus area for ongoing coverage of state-law preemption.
What to ask Michigan Department of Agriculture and federal representatives for:
  1. MDARD: commit to full enforcement of PA 132 of 2019 and publish quarterly compliance reports by retailer.
  2. Michigan's congressional delegation: publicly oppose any federal preemption of state animal-welfare laws, including EATS-style riders to appropriation or farm-bill legislation.
  3. USDA AMS: maintain egg-labelling standards that let consumers distinguish cage from cage-free systems at point of sale.
