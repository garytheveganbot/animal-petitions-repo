End the Bird Flu Amplification in US Factory Farms

Since the HPAI (H5N1) outbreak began in 2022, USDA APHIS has logged hundreds of millions of commercial and backyard birds affected by the virus — the majority inside densely-stocked commercial operations whose conditions accelerate viral spread and force mass-depopulation events.

What the data shows: USDA APHIS's commercial and backyard HPAI detections dashboard tracks every confirmed outbreak by state, flock type, and size. The running total across commercial operations represents tens of millions of birds culled or dead per outbreak year. APHIS commercial and backyard detections dashboard.
Why it keeps spreading: Animal Outlook's investigation Sick System, Sick Birds: Bird Flu & Animal Agriculture documents how the industrial model — high stocking density, ventilation dependence, monoculture genetics, long-chain supply contact — creates the ideal conditions for respiratory viruses to mutate and jump. Ventilation-shutdown depopulation, widely used during outbreaks, kills birds by heat stress over hours. Animal Outlook's investigation.
What to ask USDA APHIS for:
  1. Publish welfare and stocking-density indicators alongside HPAI detection data, so the public can see the conditions driving each outbreak.
  2. Condition outbreak-response funding on research and transition toward lower-density, higher-welfare housing.
  3. Replace ventilation-shutdown depopulation with enforceable humane-euthanasia standards as the default response, not the exception.
